Kiwimbi International

I managed a $10,000 per month Google Ad Grants account for Kiwimbi International, despite starting with no prior Google Ads experience. I taught myself the platform, stayed within grant rules, tested campaign changes over time, and helped increase website traffic by about 50 percent through this channel.

Context

Kiwimbi International is a nonprofit organization, and this work required balancing growth goals with the constraints of the Google Ad Grants program. That meant learning the platform quickly, understanding compliance rules, and improving campaign performance with limited room for error.

What I owned

I took full ownership of the Google Ad Grants account, including campaign setup, keyword research, ad writing, testing, monitoring, and ongoing optimization.

How I approached it

I started by teaching myself how Google Ads and the Ad Grants program worked. From there, I built and adjusted campaigns, tested different versions of ads and keywords, monitored performance, and kept refining the account based on what the data showed.

Results

Over time, this work increased website traffic by around 50 percent through the Google Ad Grants channel. More importantly, it showed that I can ramp up quickly, operate independently, and improve performance even when I start without a playbook.

What I learned

This experience strengthened my ability to learn fast, work through ambiguity, and use disciplined experimentation to improve results. It also confirmed that I do my best work in high-ownership environments where curiosity, structure, and persistence matter.
